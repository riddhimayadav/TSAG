College Football Recruiting Analytics

Comprehensive analysis of recruiting patterns, player development trajectories, and success prediction models for NCAA Division I football programs.

Project Overview

This extensive research project analyzes college football recruiting strategies and their correlation with on-field success. By examining recruiting rankings, player development patterns, and team performance metrics across multiple seasons, we developed predictive models to identify recruiting inefficiencies and optimal talent acquisition strategies for NCAA programs.

Critical Finding

Programs that prioritized recruiting players from specific geographic regions and high school systems showed 27% better player retention rates and 31% higher four-year graduation rates, challenging traditional star-rating focused recruiting approaches.

Research Framework

Data Collection

We compiled comprehensive datasets from recruiting services (247Sports, Rivals, ESPN), NCAA transfer portal data, academic records, and on-field performance statistics. Our analysis covered eight complete recruiting cycles (2016-2024) across all 130 FBS programs, tracking over 15,000 individual recruit trajectories.

Analytical Approach

Using machine learning algorithms and regression analysis, we developed multi-factor models predicting recruit success probability. Our framework incorporated academic metrics, geographic factors, high school competition level, physical measurables, and socioeconomic indicators to create comprehensive player profiles.

Success Metrics

We defined success through multiple lenses: on-field production (starts, statistics, awards), academic achievement (graduation rates, GPA), program retention, and NFL draft outcomes. This holistic approach provides a more complete picture of recruiting effectiveness than traditional win-loss records alone.

Impact & Future Applications

Our research provides actionable insights for college football programs seeking to optimize their recruiting strategies. The predictive models help identify undervalued prospects while highlighting potential red flags in highly-rated recruits. Several Power 5 programs have begun incorporating our methodology into their recruiting evaluation processes.

Future iterations will expand to include NIL (Name, Image, Likeness) impact analysis, transfer portal optimization strategies, and real-time recruiting trend monitoring to create a comprehensive recruiting intelligence platform for collegiate athletics.

Industry Impact

This research contributed to policy discussions at the NCAA level regarding recruiting transparency and has been cited in three academic papers on sports economics and player development in collegiate athletics.
